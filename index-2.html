This is my Journey with TELADAN Scholarship

          I am Incredibly fortunate and proud to join the Tanoto Foundation, to be part of changemakers who are generous, compassionate, and dedicated to environmental protection. This is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity that I would not have gotten anywhere else, and it is a privilege to be part of this incredible organization.

         Tanoto Foundation is a philanthropic organization founded by Sukanto Tanoto and Tinah Bingei Tanoto in 1981 in Indonesia, focusing on education, community empowerment, and improving quality of life. The foundation's mission is to reduce poverty and inequality through quality education, community development, and the development and utilization of knowledge.

         Key programs include scholarships for college students a.k.a TELADAN (Transformasi Edukasi untuk Melahirkan Pemimpin Masa Depan), professional development for teachers, and school infrastructure improvement, exemplified by the Pelita Pendidikan program which enhances education quality in rural areas. In community empowerment, the foundation supports economic development through vocational training and entrepreneurship, alongside health and nutrition initiatives like maternal and child health programs.

      Embarking on a Transformative Leadership Journey: A Self-Discovery Odyssey

          The development program accepted by each scholar is divided into several stages, starting from lead self, lead others, and professional preparation. The entire curriculum has been designed by Tanoto Foundation for its scholars from semester 2 to semester 8, aiming to create future leaders in any field with a pay-it-forward spirit, always sensitive and caring about their surroundings. 

           The 170 scholars of 2023 are divided into 10 groups (we call them tribes), and I am in Tribe 9 with 14 others from various prestigious universities in Indonesia. In this stage, we are guided by one mentor, aka Guardian Angel, named Muhammad Akkas In  along with two assistant Guardian Angels, Nizar Amir Musyaffa In  and Archela Hatikva Tjahjono In . They actively guide and observe our growth process, always providing encouragement and advice to us as scholars of 2023.
        
         I am deeply grateful to them for their guidance and mentorship over the past 18 months. They have supported me throughout my journey of self-discovery, and I have learned and grown so much thanks to them. The knowledge and experiences I have gained with them are invaluable.

           Over the course of 18 months (2023-2024), my fellow scholars and I have engaged in in-depth discussions on a range of topics designed to enhance our self-awareness, including identifying our strengths and weaknesses, understanding our personality traits, and mastering time management skills. Each month, we have various tasks that encourage self-discovery so that we become more aware of our habits and desires. These tasks come in different forms, such as texts, self-reflections, and essays, all of which help me know myself better and increase self-awareness.

      Combating Sexual Abuse with Early Sex Education: A Pay It Forward Project

            One of the most impactful Pay It Forward projects I participated in was COURSED (Combating Sexual Abuse With Early Sex Education), a collaboration between TSA USU and a local community called "Sanggar Anak Sungai Deli" (SASUDE). SASUDE provides a safe haven for children in the surrounding area to access basic education beyond their formal schooling. Some of these children have even dropped out of school, relying solely on the community for their education. SASUDE's curriculum encompasses formal subjects as well as art education, including performances and traditional dances that nurture the children's creativity beyond the confines of formal education

           In September 2023, TSA USU partnered with SASUDE to implement COURSED (Combating Sexual Abuse with Early Sex Education). This theme was chosen in response to the alarming rise in sexual abuse cases involving young children, who often lack adequate education about their own bodies. The project aimed to raise awareness and equip children with the knowledge and tools to protect themselves from sexual abuse. Considering that SASUDE caters to children aged 7-13, a period marked by curiosity and exploration, it was crucial to address this sensitive topic early on.

            I was honored to serve as one of the lead facilitators, directly engaging with the children, particularly the girls, alongside my colleagues Raissa Adilla In  and Natasha Randal Kayla Tarigan In . Prior to the on-site sessions, we underwent an online workshop to equip ourselves with the necessary knowledge and techniques to effectively teach early childhood sex education. Standing in front of a group of young children, I felt a surge of enthusiasm mixed with the challenge of delivering the material in a clear and comprehensive manner, ensuring no room for misunderstandings.

           One of the primary challenges we faced was capturing the attention of these energetic children and maintaining their focus throughout the learning process. To address this, we employed interactive strategies, such as asking them to point out objects in their surroundings, like trees or banners. This technique proved highly effective, transforming their initial distractions into active participation. We also incorporated engaging question-and-answer sessions to stimulate their critical thinking and instill confidence.

        Before and after the education sessions, we conducted brief assessments to gauge the children's comprehension of the material. The results were encouraging, indicating that they had grasped the key concepts and were more aware of their private body parts and the importance of safeguarding them from others.   
         
           In addition to raising awareness among the children, TSA USU also provided financial support to SASUDE for the construction of a new restroom facility. This donation was made possible through the generosity of public donors who wished to support the project. We facilitated the fundraising efforts and presented the funds to SASUDE representatives.

            The COURSED project was a profoundly rewarding experience that allowed me to make a meaningful contribution to the well-being of children in our community. It reinforced the importance of early sex education in empowering children to protect themselves and fostered a sense of responsibility to address this sensitive issue. I am grateful for the opportunity to have been a part of this impactful project and to have collaborated with my fellow TSA USU members in making a positive difference.

      Sharing the Spirit of Ramadan: A Micro-Project in Action

            During the holy month of Ramadan, in March 2024, I had the privilege of participating in a micro-project organized by TSA USU called "MARTABAK Ramadhan: Mari Bersama Berbagi Kemuliaan Bulan Ramadhan." This initiative aimed to foster a sense of community, promote religious tolerance, and inspire others to engage in acts of kindness and compassion.

         The project involved distributing iftar meals, consisting of rice boxes and drinks, to individuals observing the fast and those in need. The funds for this project were generously donated by members of the public and students, demonstrating the spirit of collective giving and extending the project's impact beyond our organization. With a total of Rp 1,780,000 collected, we were able to provide a substantial number of meals.
           This experience left an indelible mark on me. The opportunity to directly engage with the community and witness the realities of those around us was truly humbling. Participating in this project ignited a profound sense of gratitude within me, not only for the opportunity to connect with a diverse group of individuals but also for the surge of empathy and humility I experienced. The project's impact extended beyond those who received the meals, as it enriched my own perspective and instilled a deeper appreciation for the power of collective action.

          The MARTABAK Ramadhan project served as a reminder of the importance of compassion, generosity, and the unifying spirit of Ramadan. It highlighted the transformative power of small acts of kindness and the significance of fostering a community that embraces inclusivity and support. As I reflect on this experience, I am filled with gratitude for the opportunity to have contributed to this meaningful endeavor and to have witnessed the collective impact of our efforts.
